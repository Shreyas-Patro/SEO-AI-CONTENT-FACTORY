This guide simplifies the process, offering a clear path for **Resident Welfare Associations (RWAs), developers, and homebuyers**.\nUnlike individual houses, apartment complexes receive a single bulk water connection. BWSSB allocates water based on a standard of **200 liters per flat per day**. The RWA or developer is then responsible for the internal distribution, including managing storage tanks and pumping systems to ensure water reaches every flat.\n\n**Eligibility and Mandatory Requirements**\n\nBefore granting a connection, BWSSB requires societies to meet several prerequisites:\n**Occupancy Certificate (OC):** Confirms the building is constructed according to approved plans.\n**Rainwater Harvesting (RWH):** Mandatory for all multi-storied buildings to help replenish groundwater.\n**Sewage Treatment Plant (STP)**: Required for complexes with 120 or more flats to treat and reuse wastewater.\n**Water Conservation Measures:** Installation of tap aerators and flow limiters is compulsory.\n\n**Application and Approval Process**\nThe process involves submitting an application **(Form II NOC)** with all necessary documents. Upon approval, BWSSB issues a **demand notice** for the fees.
